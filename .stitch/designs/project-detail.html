UX/UI Case Study

Reimagining the
Digital Archive.

A deep dive into modular information architecture for curated museum collections.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

12 Weeks (Q3 2023)

Tools

Figma, Framer, Principle

Focus
Strategy Motion

The Challenge

Museum archives are often impenetrable. The sheer volume of data overwhelms the casual visitor, while the lack of visual hierarchy makes discovery difficult. How do we create a digital experience that feels as curated as a physical gallery?

Our goal was to bridge the gap between technical metadata and visual storytelling, ensuring that every artifact has the space to tell its unique story.

The Process

01. Research & Discovery

We interviewed 15 curators and 30 frequent museum-goers. The insight was clear: users wanted "guided exploration" rather than a search bar. They wanted to stumble upon connections between different historical eras.

02. Wireframing Content Clusters

The Solution.

A dynamic grid system that adapts to the content density, creating a rhythmic visual experience.

Tactile Navigation

Gestural navigation that mimics turning the pages of an art book, providing haptic feedback on every interaction.

Semantic Scale

A responsive typography system that prioritizes legibility across all screen sizes without losing its editorial soul.
